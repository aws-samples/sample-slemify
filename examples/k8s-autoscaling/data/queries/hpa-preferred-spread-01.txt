seeing weird behavior where karpenter launches 3 nodes (one per AZ) but then immediately terminates 2 of them. here's our deployment:

```yaml
apiVersion: apps/v1
kind: Deployment
metadata:
  name: api-gateway
  namespace: production
spec:
  replicas: 9
  selector:
    matchLabels:
      app: api-gateway
  template:
    metadata:
      labels:
        app: api-gateway
    spec:
      topologySpreadConstraints:
        - maxSkew: 1
          topologyKey: topology.kubernetes.io/zone
          whenUnsatisfiable: ScheduleAnyway
          labelSelector:
            matchLabels:
              app: api-gateway
      containers:
        - name: gateway
          image: api-gateway:v1.8.0
          resources:
            requests:
              cpu: "500m"
              memory: "512Mi"
            limits:
              cpu: "1"
              memory: "1Gi"
```

the HPA scales this from 3 to 30 replicas. when it scales up, karpenter provisions nodes in all 3 AZs. but because `whenUnsatisfiable: ScheduleAnyway` is a soft constraint, the kube-scheduler packs all pods onto whichever node becomes Ready first. the other 2 nodes end up empty and karpenter consolidates them within 30 seconds.

then the next scaling event happens and karpenter launches 3 more nodes, same thing happens. we're seeing nodes launch and terminate within 60 seconds, over and over.

why are my nodes launching and terminating within seconds? is this a karpenter bug or a scheduler issue? we want pods spread across zones but we also don't want pods stuck in Pending if a zone is temporarily unavailable.
