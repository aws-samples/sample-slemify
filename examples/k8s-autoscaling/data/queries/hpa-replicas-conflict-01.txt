we have a weird issue where every time we do a helm upgrade, our deployment gets reset to 5 replicas even though the HPA had scaled it to 12. here's our setup:

Deployment (in helm chart):
```yaml
apiVersion: apps/v1
kind: Deployment
metadata:
  name: web-frontend
  namespace: production
spec:
  replicas: 5
  selector:
    matchLabels:
      app: web-frontend
  template:
    metadata:
      labels:
        app: web-frontend
    spec:
      containers:
        - name: frontend
          image: web-frontend:v2.3.1
          resources:
            requests:
              cpu: "250m"
              memory: "256Mi"
            limits:
              cpu: "500m"
              memory: "512Mi"
          ports:
            - containerPort: 8080
```

HPA:
```yaml
apiVersion: autoscaling/v2
kind: HorizontalPodAutoscaler
metadata:
  name: web-frontend-hpa
  namespace: production
spec:
  scaleTargetRef:
    apiVersion: apps/v1
    kind: Deployment
    name: web-frontend
  minReplicas: 2
  maxReplicas: 20
  metrics:
    - type: Resource
      resource:
        name: cpu
        target:
          type: Utilization
          averageUtilization: 60
```

the HPA scales us up to 12 during peak traffic. but when we deploy a new version via `helm upgrade`, it resets to 5 because that's what's in the deployment spec. then the HPA has to scale back up, which takes several minutes. during that window we get 503s.

an LLM told me this is fine because the HPA will quickly scale back up. but "quickly" is like 5-10 minutes because of the stabilization window. we deploy 3-4 times a day during business hours.

is there a way to prevent helm from overriding the HPA's replica count?
